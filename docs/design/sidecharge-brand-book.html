Section 01

Brand Story

SideCharge exists to eliminate the #1 barrier to Level 2 EV charger adoption: the panel upgrade.

Why "SideCharge"?

SideCharge fuses the product's two defining traits: Side(walk)—the Amazon Sidewalk network that carries the signal—and Charge—the EV charging it enables on an existing circuit.

The name is short, memorable, and action-oriented. It works as a noun ("Check your SideCharge dashboard") and as a verb concept ("SideCharge your home"). It also carries a subtle double meaning: charging your EV on the side of an existing circuit—which is literally what the interlock does.

"Your home already has a Level 2 charger circuit—it just doesn't know it yet."

Purpose

By interlocking an EV charger with an existing HVAC circuit, SideCharge enables same-day, code-compliant Level 2 installs in homes that would otherwise require $2,400+ in electrical upgrades and days of waiting. We believe every home with central air already has a Level 2 charger circuit.

Vision

A world where every home with central air can add Level 2 EV charging the same day, with no panel upgrade, no new breaker, and no infrastructure barrier—connected and optimized over a free neighborhood network.

Mission

Deliver a code-compliant circuit interlock that shares an existing HVAC circuit with a Level 2 EV charger, connected over Amazon Sidewalk's free LoRa mesh network—eliminating panel upgrades, WiFi dependencies, and monthly fees in a single device.

Section 03

Brand Personality

SideCharge feels like a calm, clever engineer who built something genuinely useful in their garage and is quietly proud of it.

The Core Four Traits

Quietly Clever
The product does something genuinely hard but should never brag about it. Think of the friend who casually mentions they built their own solar array over the weekend.
"4 kilobytes. That's smaller than this paragraph. And it coordinates your entire home's electrical appetite."
Garage-Born, Not Corporate
A maker product that grew up. It runs on a thumb-sized radio module and talks to your EV charger through a protocol designed in the 1990s. Approachable. Real.
"Smaller than a deck of cards. Runs on less power than a phone charger."
Environmentally Aware, Not Preachy
Shifts charging to cleaner grid moments and cheaper rate windows. Present this as smart rather than virtuous. Data-driven optimism, not guilt.
"Your car charged on wind power last night."
Pleasantly Obsessive About Energy
Energy nerds are the core audience. Surface the data, show the patterns, celebrate the milestones. Warmth rather than clinical dashboards.
"Charged 34 kWh between midnight and 5am. Grid was 91% clean. Saved $4.20 vs peak rates."

Section 04

Logo

The SideCharge logo combines three visual ideas: a circuit interlock, a charging bolt, and a radio wave.

Concept

The primary visual concept is the interlock: two paths diverging from a single source, with a toggle or gate between them. One circuit, two loads, intelligently switched.

The wordmark uses Sora Bold, with the "S" or "C" potentially incorporating the toggle/switch motif as a ligature or accent.

Mark (Icon)

A stylized split path—a single line forking into two branches (HVAC and EV) with a node at the junction. Signal arcs emanate from the node for connectivity.

Conceptual direction—split-path mark with signal arcs

Color Usage Rules

1

Sidewalk Teal should represent 60% of branded surface area.

2

Electric Amber is reserved for action and attention—never use for large background fills.

3

Signal Violet is the "surprise"—use sparingly (5-10%) for delight moments.

4

Never place Amber text on Teal background—insufficient contrast ratio.

5

All text must meet WCAG 2.1 AA contrast minimums: 4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text.

6

Dark mode: swap Charcoal to backgrounds, use Mist Teal and Cloud for text.

Section 07

Voice & Tone

SideCharge speaks with confident clarity. We know the deep technical details but lead with what matters to you.

Voice Attributes

Clear
No acronyms without explanation. Short sentences. Active voice.
"SideCharge interlocks your EV charger with your AC circuit—so you can install Level 2 charging without upgrading your panel."
Confident
State capabilities directly. No hedging ("might," "up to," "possibly").
"Same-day install. No panel upgrade. Code compliant."
Warm
Conversational but not slangy. "You/your" more than "our/we."
"Your home already has the circuit. SideCharge just lets your car use it too."
Whimsical
A light touch of surprise and delight. Not forced, never corny.
"4 kilobytes. That's smaller than this paragraph."

The fifth attribute—Honest: We name limitations. We don't oversell. "Your AC and your car can't charge at the same time—but your AC runs 2 hours a day and your car charges at night, so they were never going to fight over it anyway."

Tone Shifts by Context

Marketing / Hero
Lead with the problem, then the solution
"Your panel says no. SideCharge says: you don't need to ask the panel."
Installer / Trade
Practical, code-aware, respects expertise
"Circuit interlock per CEC 440.34. One device, one visit, one invoice."

Precise, calm, functional
"HVAC: Idle. Charger: Active at 7.2 kW. Circuit: Within limits."
Error States
Reassuring, actionable—never cute
"HVAC call detected. Charging paused. Will resume when the AC cycle ends."
Utility / Grid
ROI-oriented, policy-aware
"Adds a Level 2 charger without increasing peak demand."
Social
Enthusiastic, peer-to-peer
"Level 2 charger on a 100A panel. No panel upgrade. Homeowner saved $2,400."

Words We Use & Avoid

Use These Words

  • Interlock the technical term—own it
  • Circuit sharing consumer-friendly version
  • Same-day install the outcome everyone cares about
  • Code compliant CEC 440.34
  • Smart not "AI-powered"
  • Connected not "IoT-enabled"
  • Neighborhood network for Sidewalk
  • Compact / Tiny celebrate the 4KB app

Avoid These Words

  • "Revolutionary" we are evolutionary
  • "Leverage" say "use"
  • "End-to-end" say what you mean
  • "Smart home" we are smart energy
  • "Just works" Apple owns this
  • "Seamless" say "effortless"
  • "Disruptive" overused
  • "Panel upgrade" it's always the problem

Section 12

Brand Do's & Don'ts

Guidelines for consistent brand application across all touchpoints.

Do

  • Lead with the interlock—the panel upgrade is the problem, the circuit interlock is the answer
  • Follow the interlock with "and it connects over radio, no WiFi"
  • Use real numbers ($2,400, 4KB, 15s, 14-26%)—specificity builds trust
  • Show the product in residential contexts, including electrical panels
  • Speak differently to different audiences
  • Celebrate engineering elegance (tiny footprint, delta OTA, circuit sharing)
  • Use Signal Violet as a "wink"—the unexpected detail
  • Maintain generous white space
  • Show the interlock toggle animation in product demos

Don't

  • Don't call SideCharge a "charger monitor"—it is a circuit interlock
  • Don't compare directly to ChargePoint in marketing
  • Don't use "IoT" in consumer copy (say "connected" or "smart")
  • Don't make LoRa the hero—the interlock is the hero
  • Don't place Amber text on Teal background (insufficient contrast)
  • Don't use more than 2 brand colors per card
  • Don't stack more than 3 messaging pillars per page
  • Don't bury the interlock story below connectivity features
